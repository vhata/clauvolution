# Clauvolution

An evolution simulator where you watch life emerge, adapt, compete, and speciate in real time.

## Core Vision

- [ ] **Visual simulation** — watch evolution happen in real time, not just read stats
- [ ] **Emergent speciation** — species diverge from common ancestors, no predefined species
- [ ] **Competition** — creatures compete for resources, territory, survival
- [ ] **No hard categories** — no "plant" or "animal" types; strategies emerge from evolution itself. A sessile photosynthesizer might evolve mobility. A predator might settle down and filter-feed.
- [ ] **Biomes** — oceans, deserts, jungles, etc. Different environments exert different selection pressures and drive geographic isolation
- [ ] **Niche construction** — species reshape their biomes, biomes reshape species back. Beavers build wetlands, trees turn desert to jungle, organisms terraform their own patch
- [ ] **Phenotype rendering** — creatures visually express their evolved traits. Legs, fins, gills, claws, size — you see it, not just read a tooltip
- [ ] **Spatial and temporal zoom** — pull out to watch continental-scale evolution over millennia, punch in to watch a single creature forage. Speed up to see speciation, slow down to see behaviour

## Brains

- [ ] **Per-organism neural network** — each creature has its own brain, encoded in its genome. Inherited from parents with mutation. Network topology and weights evolve under selection pressure
- [ ] **Emergent behaviour** — no designed behaviours. Hunting, fleeing, cooperating, deceiving — all emerge from evolved neural architecture
- [ ] **Cognitive speciation** — separated populations diverge cognitively as well as physically. Different environments produce different kinds of minds
- [ ] **Sentience spectrum** — as brains grow more complex, creatures exhibit learning, memory, communication, deception, play. The line between "looks purposeful" and "is purposeful" gets blurry

## Emergent Dynamics

- [ ] **Phylogenetic tree** — a living family tree alongside the simulation. Watch branches fork in real time. Trace any creature's ancestry back to the origin
- [ ] **Arms races** — co-evolution between predators and prey. Speed begets armour begets armour-cracking. Visually dramatic escalation
- [ ] **Mass extinction events** — catastrophes that wipe out dominant species and open niches for rapid diversification. Random or player-triggered
- [ ] **Convergent evolution** — unrelated lineages independently evolving similar solutions. Fins keep appearing in water. Eyes keep appearing everywhere
- [ ] **Social behaviour** — pack hunting, herding, hive structures, alarm calls, collective defence. Cooperation as an evolved strategy
- [ ] **Symbiosis** — mutualism, parasitism, commensalism. Cleaning, seed-spreading, two organisms becoming one
